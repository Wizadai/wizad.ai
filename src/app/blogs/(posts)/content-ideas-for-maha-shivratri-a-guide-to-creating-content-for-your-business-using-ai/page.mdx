Shivaratri, also known as Maha Shivaratri, is a significant Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, symbolizing the triumph of light over darkness and ignorance. The origin of Shivaratri is deeply rooted in ancient Hindu scriptures and mythology. It marks the night when Lord Shiva performed the cosmic dance of creation, preservation, and destruction, known as the Tandava. Some people also believe that it is the night when Shiva and Parvati were married. Celebrated annually on the 14th night of the waning moon in the Hindu month of Phalguna (February-March), devotees observe fasts, chant prayers, and offer milk, fruits, etc to Shiva lingams in temples. It holds profound religious and spiritual significance through self-reflection, overcoming ego, and aligning oneself with a higher consciousness. spiritual liberation.
